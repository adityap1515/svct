Srividya Charities Trust, is a registered non-profit private Trust, based at Halasuru, Bengaluru, registered under no HLS-4-00324-2022-23. The members of Srividya Charities Trust have been involved in selfless service to the needy for the past 4 decades.

Srividya Charities Trust is an endeavour to present an opportunity to like-minded people to channelise their efforts, resources and donation for the benefit of the Society.
Embracing the concept of “Vasudeiva Kutumbhakam” (“The World is One Family”), Srividya Charities Trust aims to promote happiness, peace and higher consciousness to all involved.

Annadanam (Narayan Seva)

Annadhaanam, as per Vedic Scriptures is considered the greatest of all “Dhaanams”. Annam (Food) is equated to Almighty God as per our Vedic scripts. Annadhanam to the needy, deserving is considered to be auspicious & is a charity of high order.
As prophesied by Gurus like Sri Paramaachaarya, Vallimalai Swamigal, Bhagwan Sri Satya Sai Baba and Sadhuram Swamigal, “Annadhaanam” is the core activity of Sri Vidya Charities.

Goshala- Go Grasa Seva

Every time there used to be an incident with baby Shri Krishna when Kansa sent various demons to kill him, Yashoda Maa and the people of Gokul would take him to the cowshed to get rid of any problems over Kanhaiya and to protect him. Following on from this, Pujya Bhaishri explains the importance of the holy mother cow and cow dung.
Thirty-three million demi-gods reside in mother cow (gau mata).Cow is our mother. The atmosphere of a house where there is a cow is very pure. Since all the demi-gods are present in a cow, we do not consider a cow as an ordinary animal; this is part of our culture. Similarly, Tulsi is not an ordinary plant. In our religion and culture, we also acknowledge rivers as Maa, for example Ganga Maiya, Narmada Maiya, Yamuna Maiya. We worship mountains and trees.
